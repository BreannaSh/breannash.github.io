Weight


2017
Plaster, Metal Chain, Paint, Steel Core
3.25’ x 3’ x 3’ (not including the chain to the ceiling)

Using a series of photographs, taken at a time when the body was transformed by the environmental conditions of living in Montreal, this sculpture series uses silhouettes to document a body in a certain state at a certain time because of a certain place. Reflecting on how the malleability and flexibility of a body which adapts to its surroundings silhouette photos are used as source material for an exploration of body and form. Silhouettes become source material for the creation of 3-dimensional wedges, with a focus on responding to the particular parts of the body that are selected, other materials and formal considerations play into the abstracted bodies created from this process of volleying information from the hand, through technology and back again.
